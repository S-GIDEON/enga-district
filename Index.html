Welcome to Enga Province

Enga is one of the beauitful provinces in the Papua New Guinea. It is located in the north most region of the highlands of PNG, having been
divided from the Western Highlands to become a separate province when the provinces were created at the time of independence in 1975.
Located north and east of the Southern Highlands province, Enga comprises the western half of the central plateaus. It is bounded by the
provinces of East Sepik on the north, Madang on the northeast, and Western Highlands on the east. The province consists of rugged
mountains and high-altitude valleys. You'll be impressed by Papua New Guinea's highest province filled with fast flowing rivers, spectacular
waterfalls, high valleys and mountain passes. Dominated by only one language, many people in Enga do not need to speak English or Tok
Pisin, so use a guide to ensure a smooth trip.

Wabag is the capital of Enga Province, Papua New Guinea. It is the least populous provincial capital in the country. The province has six
districts, and each district has one or more Local Level Government (LLG) areas. These include; Kandep, Kompiam Abum, Laiagam
Suirunki, Pogera Paiala, Wabag Maramuni and Wapenamanda.
